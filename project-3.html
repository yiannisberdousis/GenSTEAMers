Project 3

Ecosystem Detectives: Exploring Nature’s Balance with AI

In this project, students explore local ecosystems by building and analysing food chains. Using GenAI tools, they visualise and verify how species interact and test “what-if” scenarios, such as the loss of a predator or the introduction of a new species. Through these simulations, they observe how easily ecological balance can be disrupted. Finally, students collaborate with AI to propose and discuss solutions for protecting biodiversity, strengthening their environmental awareness, critical thinking, and responsible use of technology.

Full description

Students explore local ecosystems by learning how to create food chains and by devising hypothetical species adaptation scenarios in emergent cases. They also suggest ways to mitigate biodiversity loss.

Part 1: Students study the relationships between species and create food chains in a given or selected ecosystem. To this end, they can use Gen AI tools either to verify relations between species or to create images and/or diagrams of food chains. Alternatively, they can use a Gen AI tool to generate a food chain and then test the validity of the results. Students can ask a Gen AI tool to generate a food chain by providing the names of the species involved, and then compare the results. Teachers can support this process by providing examples of ecosystems, as well as prompts and keywords that can be used with Gen AI tools. They can also provide resources to help students evaluate the results produced.

Part 2: Using the food chains from Part 1, students create hypothetical scenarios to explore what would happen if a species stopped existing, if one part of the food chain became overpopulated, or if another species was suddenly added to the food chain (e.g. if predators travelled from other countries or continents). They can use Gen AI tools as scenario generators or thinking partners to identify the key variables affecting the food chain, and present these narratives in the classroom. They could also attempt to generate data depicting the local or global impact of such scenarios. Teachers can provide articles on climate change to help students think of such scenarios, as well as prompts to use with Gen AI tools for creating scenarios or turning them into numerical data.

Part 3: Based on the results of Parts 1 and 2, students can develop strategies to mitigate biodiversity loss. To this end, they use Gen AI tools to help them develop their strategies and consider the ethical implications. Teachers can provide examples of existing strategies. They can also encourage students to develop strategies from different standpoints and facilitate classroom debates.
