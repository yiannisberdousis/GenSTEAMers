Brief description

This project engages students in exploring how waste is generated in their daily environment and how it reflects consumption habits and behaviours. Through collecting and analysing data, students identify patterns, construct data-based narratives, and propose realistic actions for waste reduction. Using GenAI as a thinking partner, they interpret data, explore alternative solutions, and communicate evidence-based conclusions.

Full description

This interdisciplinary scenario focuses on using environmental data to support decision-making in real-world contexts. Instead of only analysing or visualising data, students are challenged to use data as evidence to justify choices and evaluate their consequences. In Part 1 (Understanding the problem context), students are introduced to a real-world environmental challenge (e.g. increasing waste in the school, rising temperatures affecting crops, or air pollution levels in a city). They are provided with a dataset related to the issue (e.g. waste quantities over time, temperature records, pollution indicators). Students first explore the data manually, identifying key patterns, trends, and possible concerns. In Part 2 (Data-informed decision making), students work in groups and are assigned roles (e.g. local authority, environmental organisation, business owner, citizen group). Each group must make a decision based on the data (e.g. introduce recycling measures, change resource use, implement restrictions). They use GenAI tools as a thinking partner and scenario generator to explore possible actions and their consequences. However, students must critically evaluate AI suggestions and ensure that all decisions are grounded in the dataset. In Part 3 (Exploring consequences and trade-offs), students use GenAI to simulate possible outcomes of their decisions (e.g. “What might happen if we reduce waste by 30%?” or “How would pollution levels change if traffic is reduced?”). They analyse different scenarios and identify trade-offs (economic, environmental, social), discussing which solution is most balanced and realistic. In Part 4 (Justification and debate), groups present and defend their decisions in a structured discussion or debate. They must justify their choices using data, explain assumptions, and respond to counterarguments from other groups.
