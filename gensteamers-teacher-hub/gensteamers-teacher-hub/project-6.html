Theme 5 Scenario 2

Chatting with AI: Uncovering the mechanisms behind chat bots

Help your students understand how AI really works by building their own chatbot. In this scenario, students first play with existing chatbots to see what they do well and where they make mistakes. Then, using simple blocks of code, students "teach" a computer to recognise information and respond to questions. By building and testing their own bots, students learn how to make technology that is helpful, unbiased, and easy to use.

Full description

Working in teams, the students interact with an AI-enhanced chatbot to identify its pros and cons. Then they create their own chatbot and discuss limitations or ethical considerations.

Part 1: The students interact with chatbots on specific webpages (e.g. a university's webpage) or with custom-made chatbots such as those on schoolhub.ai. They highlight the pros and cons of these chatbots, as well as any specific limitations or ethical concerns (biases, discriminations etc.). Teachers can provide specific prompts or a checklist for fact-checking, to help students identify these aspects.

Part 2: Using tools such as Scratch with AI extensions, the students create their own chatbots. They use tools such as Google Teachable Machine to train models that will be integrated into their chatbots. The teams then swap chatbots and assess them using a method similar to that in Part 1. Teachers can provide students with guidelines and partial solutions to help them train a model and create their own chatbot.

Part 3: Students use Gen AI tools as design coaches and thinking partners to improve their chatbots. Teachers encourage students to use Gen AI tools for debugging and to test the validity of the suggested script or code. At the end of the activity, teachers can encourage students to write a short report on their experience.

Ideas for chatbots: Chatbots can range from simple question-and-answer bots to bots that imitate historical figures and share their own views. In this case, students can also identify whether the historical information is misleading.
