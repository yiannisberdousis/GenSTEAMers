Scenario 10

Monopoly - Decoding game logic and changing the rules

Possible Project Pathway

In this scenario, students analyze the pricing of Monopoly properties to discover the mathematical formulas (functions) that drive the game. After using GenAI to model property growth and checking its accuracy, students become game co-designers. They design new rules and event cards to see how small changes in math can lead to a balanced game or a total economic collapse.

Part 1: Students explore the standard price lists of Monopoly properties to uncover the hidden mathematical logic of the game. Specifically, they analyze how rent increases as houses are added to a property. Students prompt GenAI to provide a mathematical function that models this growth (e.g., linear or exponential growth). They then manually verify this function (or use software like GeoGebra) to see if the AI’s model accurately predicts the rent of a hotel. Teachers provide prompts that encourage students to investigate the AI’s accuracy and lead a discussion on whether this pricing model is designed for a fair or balanced game, or if it intentionally pushes players toward bankruptcy.

Part 2: Students take the role of game designers by exploring a what-if scenario. They design a new event card that alters a specific variable in the original function (e.g., the Housing Crisis card that increases all multipliers by 20%). Students use GenAI as a thinking partner to brainstorm different rules and ask the tool to generate a new function based on these changes.

Part 3: Using the visual data from their new models, students debate whether their proposed changes keep the game viable or if the new parameter—or new mathematical reality—would end the game for all players immediately.
