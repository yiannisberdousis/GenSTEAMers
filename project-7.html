Project 7

Mapping the Time: Daily Journeys in Data

In this scenario, students analyze a daily activity, such as their journey to school. They begin by collecting and manually calculating their own data (e.g., time, distance, and transport) to understand the numbers. Then, they use GenAI as a data mapping tool to visualize their findings, spot trends, and cross-check the results for accuracy. Finally, students act as conceptual artists, collaborating with AI to transform their strict geographic data into a unique, artistic map (such as a Time-Map) of their city.

Full description

Students explore their daily journey to school. They collect "Time-Data" (how long it takes using different routes or transport) and use GenAI to help model, visualize, and eventually map this data artistically.

Part 1: Over a specific time period (e.g. one week), students record their commute time and try to compare two variables (e.g. Route 1 vs Route 2, or Walking vs Taking the bus). They then manually calculate the mean, median and range of their journey times. Teachers can provide a logbook template to help students collect the data and ensure they understand the process before using a Gen AI tool.

Part 2: Students provide data to a Gen AI tool and ask it to create a specific graph and a brief summary of the trends in commuting. They then cross-check the results. For example, they check whether the mean or median calculated by the AI matches their own manual calculation, and whether there is a difference in the scale of the AI's graph that might affect the way the data is perceived. Teachers help students identify whether the AI-generated graphs are accurate or include misleading data not present in the original numbers.

Part 3: Using the data, the students attempt to create a conceptual time map of their commute. They can use Gen AI as a creative consultant to help them transform a simple map into an artistic data map. Consulting the art teacher, STEM teachers can provide students with ideas on how to create such a conceptual map. They can also inspire imagination by inviting them to contribute to a collective piece of work titled Hidden Time-Geography of the City.
