Full description

This project introduces students to the concept of the water footprint, helping them understand how everyday choices—such as food, clothing, and consumer products—are connected to water use in often invisible ways. Through a combination of data exploration, modelling, and reflection, students develop a more informed and critical perspective on resource consumption and sustainability.

Part 1 – Exploring Visible and Hidden Water Use: Students explore their own daily water use and are introduced to the concept of hidden water in everyday products. Using GenAI as a question generator and explanation assistant, they investigate production processes and compare AI-generated explanations with reliable sources.

Part 2 – Working with Water Footprint Data: Students analyse datasets showing the water footprint of common items, identifying patterns, comparisons, and extreme values. GenAI supports this process as a pattern-spotter and estimation assistant, while students critically evaluate its suggestions.

Part 3 – Building a Personal or Group Water Footprint Profile: Students create a simple personal or group model based on their habits and represent it using tables, graphs, or infographics. GenAI acts as a visualisation assistant and thinking partner to support data representation.

Part 4 – Interpreting and Questioning the Data: Students interpret their results, identify surprising findings, and question assumptions. They explore alternative scenarios (e.g. different contexts or behaviours), using GenAI as a scenario generator.

Part 5 – From Awareness to Informed Decisions: Students propose realistic actions to reduce their water footprint and evaluate their impact using data. They present evidence-based conclusions and reflect on the role of assumptions, context, and data limitations in decision-making.
