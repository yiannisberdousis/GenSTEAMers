💧 Sample Project

Investigating Our Water Footprint

How much water do we really use every day, and how much of it is hidden in the products we consume?

💡 Big Idea

Water is not only what we see. It is also hidden in food, clothing, and everyday products. This project helps students uncover those invisible connections and use evidence to reflect on choices and possible actions.

🤖 Where GenAI comes in

  • Question generator
  • Explanation assistant
  • Pattern-spotter
  • Visualisation assistant
  • Thinking partner

🧠 What students do

  • Identify visible and hidden water use in daily life.
  • Explore water footprint data for food, clothes, and common products.
  • Compare examples and detect surprising patterns.
  • Build a personal or group water footprint profile.
  • Reflect on informed and realistic changes.

🔄 Learning Journey

1. Discover hidden water
2. Analyse data
3. Build a model
4. Interpret results
5. Propose action

🎓 Student Output

  • Personal or group water footprint model
  • Graphs, tables, or a simple infographic
  • Evidence-based suggestions to reduce water use

✨ Imagine this in your classroom

Students realise that a simple T-shirt or cup of coffee may require far more water than expected. They compare habits, question assumptions, and begin to see sustainability as something connected to everyday decisions.
