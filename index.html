Project 1

Our Water Footprint: uncovering hidden water in everyday choices

Students explore the concept of the water footprint by uncovering the hidden water behind everyday choices such as food, clothing, and products. Through analysing real-world data and building their own simplified models, they identify patterns, question assumptions, and reflect on how consumption connects to resource use. Using GenAI as a thinking partner, students generate questions, interpret data, and explore alternative scenarios, leading to more informed and evidence-based decisions about sustainability.

4–5 weeks (approximately 2 hours per week) ChatGPT

Project 2

The Energy Synthesis: A Student-AI Collaborative journey

Challenge your students to become Energy researchers and architects. This scenario helps students explore the science of energy transformation. They begin by researching energy origins using GenAI tools and move toward debating sustainable solutions for their school or local community. Finally, students prove their ideas by using AI as a thinking partner and technical consultant to design and create a physical or digital functional prototype (such as a wind turbine or solar-powered circuit).

5–6 weeks (2 hours per week) Gemini
Open project
Project 3

Ecosystem Detectives: Exploring Nature’s Balance with AI

In this project, students explore local ecosystems by building and analysing food chains. Using GenAI tools, they visualise and verify how species interact and test “what-if” scenarios, such as the loss of a predator or the introduction of a new species. Through these simulations, they observe how easily ecological balance can be disrupted. Finally, students collaborate with AI to propose and discuss solutions for protecting biodiversity, strengthening their environmental awareness, critical thinking, and responsible use of technology.

4–6 weeks (2 hours per week) ChatGPT
Open project
Project 4

Our Waste Story: uncovering patterns and driving change through data

This project engages students in exploring how waste is generated in their daily environment and how it reflects consumption habits and behaviours. Through collecting and analysing data, students identify patterns, construct data-based narratives, and propose realistic actions for waste reduction. Using GenAI as a thinking partner, they interpret data, explore alternative solutions, and communicate evidence-based conclusions.

4–5 weeks (2 hours per week) ChatGPT

Project 6

Chatting with AI: Uncovering the mechanisms behind chat bots

Help your students understand how AI really works by building their own chatbot. In this scenario, students first play with existing chatbots to see what they do well and where they make mistakes. Then, using simple blocks of code, students "teach" a computer to recognise information and respond to questions. By building and testing their own bots, students learn how to make technology that is helpful, unbiased, and easy to use.

4–6 weeks (2 hours per week) schoolhub.ai
Open project
Project 7

Mapping the Time: Daily Journeys in Data

In this scenario, students analyze a daily activity, such as their journey to school. They begin by collecting and manually calculating their own data (e.g., time, distance, and transport) to understand the numbers. Then, they use GenAI as a data mapping tool to visualize their findings, spot trends, and cross-check the results for accuracy. Finally, students act as conceptual artists, collaborating with AI to transform their strict geographic data into a unique, artistic map (such as a Time-Map) of their city.

4–5 weeks (2 hours per week) Gemini
Open project
Project 8

Our Screen Time: uncovering patterns and challenging assumptions

Students analyse screen time data from their own digital habits to identify patterns, detect anomalies, and draw evidence-based conclusions. Through working with real or anonymised datasets, they explore how and when devices are used, question assumptions, and compare different interpretations. Using GenAI as a thinking partner, students generate hypotheses, examine alternative explanations, and develop a more critical and informed understanding of their digital behaviour.

4–5 weeks (2 hours per week) ChatGPT
Open project
Project 9

From virtual lab to a real-world system

Students explore how environmental data can inform real-world decisions by using GenAI as a virtual lab to simulate classroom conditions such as temperature and humidity. They visualise and critically analyse the generated data, identifying inconsistencies and reflecting on what reliable, realistic data should look like. Building on these insights, they design their own monitoring system, using AI as a thinking partner to propose practical, evidence-based solutions while developing data literacy, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills.

5 weeks (2 hours per week) Gemini
Open project
Project 10

Monopoly - Decoding game logic and changing the rules

In this scenario, students analyze the pricing of Monopoly properties to discover the mathematical formulas (functions) that drive the game. After using GenAI to model property growth and checking its accuracy, students become game co-designers. They design new rules and event cards to see how small changes in math can lead to a balanced game or a total economic collapse.

3–4 weeks (2 hours per week) ChatGPT
