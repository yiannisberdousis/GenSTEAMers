Theme 1 Scenario 1

Our Water Footprint: uncovering hidden water in everyday choices

Students explore the concept of the water footprint by uncovering the hidden water behind everyday choices such as food, clothing, and products.

Brief description

Through analysing real-world data and building their own simplified models, students identify patterns, question assumptions, and reflect on how consumption connects to resource use. Using GenAI as a thinking partner, they generate questions, interpret data, and explore alternative scenarios, leading to more informed and evidence-based decisions about sustainability.

Indicative tools

  • ChatGPT
  • Gemini
  • Canva
  • Claude

Estimated time: 4–5 weeks (approximately 2 hours per week)

Suggested learning flow

1. Discover hidden water
2. Analyse examples
3. Build a simple model
4. Interpret patterns
5. Propose action

Theme 2 Scenario 2

The AI Energy Quest: in search of the Energy Shift

Students explore the science of energy transformation and move from research and debate to design and prototyping.

Brief description

Students begin by researching energy origins using GenAI tools and move toward debating sustainable solutions for their school or local community. They then use AI as a thinking partner and technical consultant to design and create a physical or digital functional prototype, such as a wind turbine or a solar-powered circuit.

Indicative tools

  • Gemini
  • ChatGPT
  • Canva

Estimated time: 5–6 weeks

Theme 5 Scenario 2

Chatting with AI: Uncovering the mechanisms behind chatbots

Students investigate how AI really works by building and testing their own chatbot systems.

Brief description

Students first explore existing chatbots to understand their strengths and weaknesses. Then, using simple blocks of code, they teach a computer to recognise information and respond to questions. By building and testing their own bots, they reflect on helpfulness, bias, and usability.

Indicative tools

  • schoolhub.ai
  • Gemini
  • Scratch
  • Teachable Machine

Estimated time: 4–6 weeks

Theme 6 Scenario 2

Mapping the Time: Daily Journeys in Data

Students collect and map data from daily activity patterns such as their journey to school.

Brief description

Students begin by collecting and manually calculating their own data, such as time, distance, and transport. They then use GenAI as a data mapping tool to visualise findings, spot trends, and cross-check results for accuracy. Finally, they transform their structured geographic data into a creative artistic map of their city.

Indicative tools

  • Gemini
  • ChatGPT
  • Canva
  • DALL·E

Estimated time: 4–5 weeks

Theme 9 Scenario 3

Monopoly - Decoding game logic and changing the rules

Students analyse the pricing logic of Monopoly and redesign the game through mathematical modelling.

Brief description

Students analyse the pricing of Monopoly properties to discover the mathematical formulas that drive the game. After using GenAI to model property growth and checking its accuracy, they become game co-designers and create new rules and event cards to test how small mathematical changes affect balance and collapse.

Indicative tools

  • Gemini
  • ChatGPT

Estimated time: 3–4 weeks
