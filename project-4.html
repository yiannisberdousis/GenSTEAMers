Full description

This project introduces students to the analysis of everyday waste as a real-world context for developing data literacy, critical thinking, and environmental awareness. Through observation, data analysis, and communication, students move from understanding waste production to proposing informed actions.

Part 1 – Understanding Waste in Context: Students observe how waste is generated in their daily environment (school, home, community) and identify different types of waste. They formulate initial questions about patterns of waste production. Using GenAI as an explanation assistant and question generator, students explore material lifecycles and environmental impact, while critically evaluating AI-generated information.

Part 2 – Working with Waste Data: Students collect or analyse datasets related to waste (e.g. quantities, categories, time patterns). They organise data, create visualisations, and identify trends and anomalies. GenAI supports this process as a data interpretation assistant and visualisation coach, suggesting alternative representations and explanations that students verify using evidence.

Part 3 – Building Environmental Data Stories: Students construct data-based narratives that explain what is happening, why it is happening, and what the data reveals about behaviour. GenAI is used as a thinking partner and co-writer, helping students structure their narratives while ensuring that all claims are grounded in data.

Part 4 – Designing Evidence-Based Actions: Students identify key issues emerging from their data and propose realistic actions to reduce waste or improve practices. GenAI acts as a design assistant, supporting the exploration of alternative solutions. Students evaluate the feasibility and potential impact of their proposals using their data.

Part 5 – Communicating to an Audience: Students present their findings and proposals to a real audience (e.g. school community or local stakeholders), clearly communicating data and justifying their conclusions. GenAI supports this phase as a communication assistant, helping structure arguments while maintaining clarity and accuracy.
