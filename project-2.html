Scenario 2

The Energy Synthesis: A Student-AI Collaborative journey

Challenge your students to become Energy researchers and architects. This scenario helps students explore the science of energy transformation. They begin by researching energy origins using GenAI tools and move toward debating sustainable solutions for their school or local community. Finally, students prove their ideas by using AI as a thinking partner and technical consultant to design and create a physical or digital functional prototype (such as a wind turbine or solar-powered circuit).

Brief description

Finally, students prove their ideas by using AI as a thinking partner and technical consultant to design and create a physical or digital functional prototype (such as a wind turbine or solar-powered circuit).

Full description

Students will become familiar with concepts related to different forms of energy and how they transform. They will also learn to justify why a specific form of energy is more efficient in a given scenario. Optionally, they will also create a robotic/electronic artefact to support their opinion.

Part 1: Using Gen AI tools, students can explore the origins of a specific form of energy, its everyday applications, and the consequences of its potential disappearance. Teachers can provide explicit instructions on the types of prompts that could be used, as well as materials such as videos or documents to help students cross-check the validity of the responses. Teachers can also provide prompts or keywords to help students create relevant visual materials.

Part 2: After Part 1, students will become familiar with the processes of energy transformation through given examples and learn to describe such phenomena scientifically. To this end, they can enter descriptions of these scenarios into Gen AI tools and check the results. Using a checklist provided by teachers, students can assess whether the results are scientifically accurate and refine the content accordingly.

Part 3: Students are split into teams and asked to consider how renewable power could facilitate an operation in a school building. They use Gen AI tools to help them develop their ideas and present them in the plenary, highlighting pros and cons. Teachers can also encourage some teams to support opposing opinions using available data and hold a debate. Teachers can also ask students to create mock-ups using simple electronics to represent their ideas, thus testing the validity of Gen AI's suggestions.

Part 4: Students are using Gen AI as a thinking partner and design consultant to help them create a robotic artefact that brings their idea to life (e.g. a DIY wind turbine). They need to write appropriate prompts to find out which electronic components to use, how to create the circuit and how to program the artefact (if needed). Teachers help students with the prompts and provide the necessary equipment for the artefacts. Teachers also encourage students to develop their artefacts and present their creations in the plenary. Tip: If no physical equipment is available, then simulators such as TinkerCAD can be used instead.
